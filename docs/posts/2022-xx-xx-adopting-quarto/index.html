Background

I have blogged intermittently since 2014. My original blog was in WordPress with the beautiful and sparse tru theme. Writing prose and including images was not difficult, but including R code was clunky at best. And I was unable to resolve the issues that prevented my blog from being included in R-bloggers.

When I discovered the distill package, I drafted some blog posts in rmarkdown and published on GitHub pages. There were some bumpy spots and workarounds, but creating R-focused posts was straightforward and comfortable given my experience with reproducible authoring using rmarkdown, knitr, etc.

As an aside, I experimented with blogdown at the same time I tried distill. I’m certain that blogdown works beautifully for many folks, but for me, distill took me farther faster with fewer hangups.

I enjoyed writing with distill. The only significant issue was, again, an inability to create a full RSS feed suitable for submitting to R-bloggers (#376).

Looking for suggestions on fixing the RSS feed, I came across a similar discussion on a quarto page. One of the contributors (thanks Bryan!) had a distill workaround but suggested I take a look at quarto. I did. He was right. Eventually—with some expeditious bug fixing (#946)—I could use quarto to create a blog (this site) that met the requirements for aggregation on R-bloggers.

Get started with quarto

The getting started with quarto guidelines have been done. I’ll just point you to the readings I found helpful.

  • We don’t talk about Quarto by Alison Hill
  • Porting a distill blog to quarto by Danielle Navarro
  • Notes on changing from Rmarkdown/Bookdown to Quarto by Nick Tierney
  • Get started on the quarto website

The quarto features I like, compared to distill, are

  • _metadata.yml for applying settings to all posts, shortening the YAML header of individual posts
  • _quarto.yml with the usual website organization structure plus additional formatting applied to all posts
  • Full RSS feed with categories and an XML file that passes muster with an RSS feed checker.
